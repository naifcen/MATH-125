Problem 3: Mean

Calculate the mean of the following commuting times (in minutes) for a random sample of 15 NC workers.
20, 35, 8, 70, 5, 15, 25, 30, 40, 35, 10, 12, 40, 15, 20

Show Solution

Calculation:

Sum of all times = 380 minutes.

Number of workers (n) = 15.

Mean (x̄) = 380 / 15 = 25.33 minutes.

Problem 4: Quartiles & Outliers

Using the NC commuting times from the previous problem, find the quartiles and identify any outliers.

Show Solution

Step 1: Order the data.

5, 8, 10, 12, 15, 15, 20, 20, 25, 30, 35, 35, 40, 40, 70

Step 2: Find Median and Quartiles.

  • Median (M or Q₂): The 8th value is 20.
  • Q₁ (Median of lower half): The 4th value of (5, 8, 10, 12, 15, 15, 20) is 12.
  • Q₃ (Median of upper half): The 4th value of (25, 30, 35, 35, 40, 40, 70) is 35.

Step 3: Identify Outliers.

  • IQR = Q₃ - Q₁ = 35 - 12 = 23.
  • 1.5 * IQR = 1.5 * 23 = 34.5.
  • Lower Bound: 12 - 34.5 = -22.5.
  • Upper Bound: 35 + 34.5 = 69.5.
  • The value 70 is greater than the upper bound of 69.5, so it is a suspected outlier.

Problem 5: Standard Deviation

What is the variability (standard deviation) of weights (pounds) among this sample of seven neighborhood cats?
8.4, 11.7, 10.2, 9.5, 12.3, 7.9, 13.5

Show Solution

Step 1: Calculate the mean (x̄).

Mean = 73.5 / 7 = 10.5 pounds.

Step 2: Calculate Deviations and Squared Deviations.

  • (8.4 - 10.5)² = 4.41
  • (11.7 - 10.5)² = 1.44
  • (10.2 - 10.5)² = 0.09
  • (9.5 - 10.5)² = 1.0
  • (12.3 - 10.5)² = 3.24
  • (7.9 - 10.5)² = 6.76
  • (13.5 - 10.5)² = 9.0

Step 3: Calculate Variance (s²).

Sum of squared deviations = 25.94.

s² = 25.94 / (7 - 1) = 25.94 / 6 ≈ 4.32

Step 4: Calculate Standard Deviation (s).

s = √4.32 ≈ 2.08 pounds.
