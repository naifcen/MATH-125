Statistics Flashcards

Review all concepts, symbols, and formulas from Chapters 1 & 2.

What are 'Individuals' in statistics?

Show Answer

The objects (people, animals, things) described by a set of data.

What is a 'Variable'?

Show Answer

A characteristic of an individual.

Define 'Population'.

Show Answer

The entire set of individuals we are interested in studying.

Define 'Sample'.

Show Answer

A subset of individuals drawn from a population, used to draw conclusions about the whole population.

What is a 'Population Parameter'?

Show Answer

A measurable characteristic of a population. It's denoted by Greek letters.

What is a 'Sample Statistic'?

Show Answer

A measurable characteristic of a sample. It's denoted by Latin letters.

What does the symbol μ represent?

Show Answer

The Population Mean.

What does the symbol σ represent?

Show Answer

The Population Standard Deviation.

What does the symbol x̄ represent?

Show Answer

The Sample Mean.

What does the symbol 's' represent?

Show Answer

The Sample Standard Deviation.

Define 'Categorical Variable'.

Show Answer

A variable that places individuals into one of several groups or categories. Plotted with pie charts or bar graphs.

Define 'Quantitative Variable'.

Show Answer

A variable that takes numerical values for which arithmetic operations make sense. Plotted with histograms or stemplots.

What does the acronym SOCS stand for?

Show Answer

Shape, Outlier, Center, Spread. This is used to describe the overall pattern of a distribution.

Describe a 'Right-Skewed' distribution.

Show Answer

The right side (tail) of the graph is much longer than the left side. For this shape, the Mean > Median.

Describe a 'Left-Skewed' distribution.

Show Answer

The left side (tail) of the graph is much longer than the right side. For this shape, the Mean < Median.

What is the key difference between a histogram and a bar graph?

Show Answer

A histogram displays the distribution of a quantitative variable (bars usually touch), while a bar graph displays the distribution of a categorical variable (bars have spaces).

Which measure of center is 'resistant' and what does that mean?

Show Answer

The Median. 'Resistant' means it is not influenced by extreme observations (outliers). The Mean is not resistant.

What is the 'Interquartile Range' (IQR) and what does it measure?

Show Answer

IQR is the distance between the first (Q₁) and third (Q₃) quartiles. It is a resistant measure of the variability of the central 50% of the data.

List the components of the 'Five-Number Summary'.

Show Answer

Minimum, First Quartile (Q₁), Median (M), Third Quartile (Q₃), Maximum.

What does 'Standard Deviation' measure?

Show Answer

It measures the typical or average distance of observations from the sample mean (x̄). It is the most common measure of variability but is NOT resistant.

When should you use the Mean/SD vs. the Five-Number Summary?

Show Answer

Use Mean and Standard Deviation (s) for reasonably symmetric distributions without outliers. Use the Five-Number Summary for skewed distributions or distributions with strong outliers.

What is the formula for the 'Mean' (x̄)?

Show Answer

x̄ = (Σxᵢ) / n

What is the formula for the 'IQR'?

Show Answer

IQR = Q₃ - Q₁

What is the rule/formula for identifying 'Outliers'?

Show Answer

An observation is an outlier if it falls outside the following bounds:

Lower Bound: Q₁ - (1.5 × IQR)

Upper Bound: Q₃ + (1.5 × IQR)

What is the formula for 'Variance' (s²)?

Show Answer

s² = Σ(xᵢ - x̄)² / (n-1)
