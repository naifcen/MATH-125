Problem 2: Interpreting a Regression Model

[cite_start]

The relationship between class absences ($x$) and final grades ($y$) is illustrated by the regression model below[cite: 585]:
$\hat{y} = 94.332 - 5.710x$

Tasks:
a. [cite_start]What does the intercept mean in this context? [cite: 596]
b. [cite_start]What does the slope mean in this context? [cite: 597]
c. [cite_start]If a student misses three (3) class sessions, what is their predicted final score? [cite: 598]
d. [cite_start]Another student's final score was 82. If they also missed 3 classes, did they perform better or worse than predicted? [cite: 599]

Show Solution

a. Intercept (94.332): The predicted final grade for a student with **zero absences** is 94.332.

b. Slope (-5.710): For each **additional class a student misses**, their final grade is predicted to **decrease by 5.710 points**.

c. Prediction for x=3:
$\hat{y} = 94.332 - 5.710(3)$
$\hat{y} = 94.332 - 17.13 = 77.192$
The predicted final score is **77.202**.

d. Performance vs. Prediction:
The student's residual is: observed - predicted = $82 - 77.202 = +4.798$.
Since the residual is positive, the student performed **better than predicted**.

Problem 3: Coffee Shop Sales Model

A coffee shop wants to understand the relationship between hours open ($x$) and daily sales ($y$). [cite_start]They have the following summary statistics[cite: 601, 602]:

  • Mean Hours ($\bar{x}$): 7
  • Std. Dev. of Hours ($s_x$): 1.49
  • Mean Sales ($\bar{y}$): $179
  • Std. Dev. of Sales ($s_y$): $34.79
  • Correlation ($r$): 0.99

Tasks:
a. [cite_start]What percent of variation in sales is explained by the hours open? [cite: 604]
b. [cite_start]Calculate the slope ($b$) and intercept ($a$) of the least-squares regression line[cite: 605, 606].
c. [cite_start]Write the final regression equation[cite: 607].

Show Solution

a. Percent of Variation ($r^2$):

$r^2 = (0.99)^2 = 0.9801$.
So, **98.01%** of the variation in daily sales is explained by the number of hours the shop is open.

b. Slope and Intercept Calculation:

Slope ($b$) = $r \frac{s_y}{s_x} = 0.99 \times \frac{34.79}{1.49} \approx 23.09$

Intercept ($a$) = $\bar{y} - b\bar{x} = 179 - (23.09 \times 7) = 179 - 161.63 \approx 17.37$

c. Regression Equation:

$\hat{y} = 17.37 + 23.09x$
