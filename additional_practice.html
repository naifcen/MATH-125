Exercise 1: Ages of Charity Run Participants

The following data represents the ages of 20 randomly selected participants in a local 5k charity run:

21, 45, 33, 28, 19, 24, 38, 41, 22, 29, 35, 26, 20, 48, 30, 25, 28, 39, 23, 31

Task: Create a frequency table and describe the shape of the distribution using SOCS (Shape, Outlier, Center, Spread).

1. Ordered Data: `19, 20, 21, 22, 23, 24, 25, 26, 28, 28, 29, 30, 31, 33, 35, 38, 39, 41, 45, 48`

2. Frequency Table (using bins of width 10):

Age BinFrequency
10-191
20-299
30-396
40-494

3. SOCS Description:

  • Shape: The distribution is skewed to the right. The majority of runners are in their 20s, with a tail extending into the older age groups.
  • Outlier: There are no obvious outliers.
  • Center: The center (median) is around 28-29 years old.
  • Spread: The ages range from 19 to 48, a spread of 29 years.

Exercise 2: Effect of an Outlier

The monthly salaries of 6 employees at a small startup are:

$4000, $4200, $4500, $4800, $5000, $15000

Task: Calculate both the mean and the median salary. Which is the better measure of the "typical" salary at this company, and why?

Mean Calculation: (4000 + 4200 + 4500 + 4800 + 5000 + 15000) / 6 = 37500 / 6 = $6250.

Median Calculation: The data is already ordered. Since n=6 (even), we average the two middle values: (4500 + 4800) / 2 = $4650.

Conclusion:

The **median ($4650)** is the better measure of a typical salary. The mean ($6250) is significantly higher because it is skewed by the single high salary of $15,000. The median is resistant to this outlier and gives a more accurate representation of what most employees earn.

Exercise 3: Smartphone Battery Life

A tech blog tests the battery life (in hours) of 11 different smartphone models with the following results:

8.5, 9.1, 9.4, 9.5, 9.8, 10.2, 10.5, 10.8, 11.2, 11.5, 14.5

Task: Find the five-number summary and use the 1.5 x IQR rule to identify any suspected outliers.

1. Five-Number Summary:

  • Minimum: 8.5
  • Median (M): n=11 (odd), so the (11+1)/2 = 6th value is the median. M = 10.2
  • First Quartile (Q₁): The median of the lower half (`8.5, 9.1, 9.4, 9.5, 9.8`). Q₁ = 9.4
  • Third Quartile (Q₃): The median of the upper half (`10.5, 10.8, 11.2, 11.5, 14.5`). Q₃ = 11.2
  • Maximum: 14.5

2. Outlier Check:

  • IQR: Q₃ - Q₁ = 11.2 - 9.4 = 1.8
  • 1.5 x IQR: 1.5 * 1.8 = 2.7
  • Lower Bound: Q₁ - 2.7 = 9.4 - 2.7 = 6.7
  • Upper Bound: Q₃ + 2.7 = 11.2 + 2.7 = 13.9

Conclusion: The value **14.5** is greater than the upper bound of 13.9, so it is a suspected outlier.
