Part 1: Key Concepts from Chapter 1

1.1 Foundational Terminology

Individuals & Variable

Individuals: The objects described by a set of data.

Variable: A characteristic of an individual.

Population & Sample

Population: The complete set of individuals in a study.

Sample: A subset of individuals drawn from the population to make inferences about the whole.

1.2 Parameters vs. Statistics

Population Parameter

A measurable characteristic of a population.

Denoted by Greek letters (e.g., μ, σ, ρ).

Sample Statistic

A measurable characteristic of a sample.

Denoted by Latin letters (e.g., x̄, s, r).

1.3 Types of Variables

Categorical Variable

Assigns individuals to groups or categories.

Graphs: Pie Charts, Bar Graphs.

Quantitative Variable

Takes numerical values where arithmetic is meaningful.

Graphs: Histograms, Stemplots.

1.4 Exploratory Data Analysis (SOCS)

When examining a graph, always describe the following:

  • Shape: Symmetric, Skewed Right, or Skewed Left.
  • Outlier: Any values that deviate from the overall pattern.
  • Center: The midpoint of the data.
  • Spread: The variability or range of the data.

Part 2: Key Concepts from Chapter 2

2.1 Measures of Center

Mean (x̄)

The arithmetic average of the data.

x̄ = (Σxᵢ) / n

⚠️ Not Resistant: Heavily influenced by outliers.

Median (M)

The midpoint of an ordered dataset.

Location = (n + 1) / 2

✅ Resistant: Not affected by outliers.

Mean vs. Median in Skewed Distributions

Right-Skewed: Mean > Median

Left-Skewed: Mean < Median

Symmetric: Mean ≈ Median

2.2 Measures of Variability (Spread)

Quartiles & IQR

Quartiles (Q₁, Q₃): Values that divide the data into four equal parts.

Interquartile Range (IQR): A resistant measure of spread for the middle 50% of data.

IQR = Q₃ - Q₁

Standard Deviation (s)

The most common measure of spread, representing the typical distance of an observation from the mean.

s = √[ Σ(xᵢ - x̄)² / (n-1) ]

⚠️ Not Resistant: Heavily influenced by outliers.

2.3 Summaries and Plots

The Five-Number Summary & Boxplot

A resistant summary of the data, visualized by a boxplot.

Minimum | Q₁ | Median | Q₃ | Maximum

Identifying Outliers (1.5 x IQR Rule)

An observation is a suspected outlier if it is outside these bounds:

Lower Bound: Q₁ - (1.5 × IQR)

Upper Bound: Q₃ + (1.5 × IQR)

Show Solution

Exercise 3: Five-Number Summary & Outliers

Number of minutes 12 customers spent in a store: 15, 41, 12, 18, 22, 25, 17, 33, 21, 28, 9, 39

a) Find the five-number summary.

b) Calculate the IQR.

c) Determine if there are any outliers.

Show Solution

Ordered Data: 9, 12, 15, 17, 18, 21, 22, 25, 28, 33, 39, 41

a) Five-Number Summary:

  • Minimum: 9
  • Q₁: (15+17)/2 = 16
  • Median: (21+22)/2 = 21.5
  • Q₃: (28+33)/2 = 30.5
  • Maximum: 41

b) IQR: 30.5 - 16 = 14.5

c) Outliers:

  • Lower Bound: 16 - (1.5 * 14.5) = -5.75
  • Upper Bound: 30.5 + (1.5 * 14.5) = 52.25
  • Conclusion: No outliers, as all data points fall within the bounds.
