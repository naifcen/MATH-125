Statistics Symbols & Formulas

A quick reference guide for Chapters 1, 2, 4 & 5.

Key Symbols

A critical concept in statistics is distinguishing between a measure for a whole **population** (a parameter) and a measure for a **sample** (a statistic).

Population Parameters (Greek Letters)

$\mu$Population Mean (mu)

$\sigma$Population Standard Deviation (sigma)

$\rho$Population Correlation Coefficient (rho)

Sample Statistics (Latin Letters)

$\bar{x}$Sample Mean (x-bar)

$s$Sample Standard Deviation

$s^2$Sample Variance

$r$Sample Correlation Coefficient

$\hat{y}$Predicted Response Value (y-hat)

Other Common Symbols

$M$Median

$Q_1, Q_3$First and Third Quartiles

$n$Number of observations in a sample

Mathematical Notations

Summation Notation ($\Sigma x_i$)

A shorthand way to express the sum of all values in a dataset.

$\Sigma x_i = x_1 + x_2 + ... + x_n$

$\Sigma$ (Sigma): Means "sum up" or "add all."

$x_i$: Represents each individual observation in the dataset, from the first ($x_1$) to the last ($x_n$).

Chapters 1 & 2 Formulas

Sample Mean ($\bar{x}$)

The arithmetic average of the data.

$\bar{x} = \frac{\Sigma x_i}{n}$

Median Location

Finds the position of the median in an ordered dataset.

$\frac{n + 1}{2}$

Interquartile Range (IQR)

Measures the spread of the middle 50% of the data.

IQR = $Q_3 - Q_1$

Sample Variance ($s^2$)

The average of the squared deviations from the mean.

$s^2 = \frac{\Sigma(x_i - \bar{x})^2}{n-1}$

Sample Standard Deviation ($s$)

The square root of the variance. Measures the typical distance from the mean.

$s = \sqrt{s^2}$

Outlier Fences (1.5 x IQR Rule)

Calculates the boundaries for identifying suspected outliers.

Lower Bound: $Q_1 - (1.5 \times IQR)$

Upper Bound: $Q_3 + (1.5 \times IQR)$

Chapters 4 & 5 Formulas

Correlation Coefficient ($r$)

Measures strength and direction of a linear relationship.

$r = \frac{1}{n-1}\Sigma (\frac{x_i - \bar{x}}{s_x})(\frac{y_i - \bar{y}}{s_y})$

Least-Squares Regression Line (LSRL)

The model used for prediction.

$\hat{y} = a + bx$

Slope of LSRL ($b$)

Calculates the rate of change.

$b = r \frac{s_y}{s_x}$

Intercept of LSRL ($a$)

The predicted starting value of y when x=0.

$a = \bar{y} - b\bar{x}$

Residual

The difference between observed and predicted values (prediction error).

Residual = $y - \hat{y}$

Coefficient of Determination ($r^2$)

The percent of variation in y explained by the model.

$r^2 = (\text{correlation})^2$
